Short-form video is the dominant marketing channel for small businesses in 2026. Instagram Reels, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts generate higher organic reach than any other content format — and the algorithm actively promotes small, authentic business accounts over polished corporate content.

The problem is production. Creating quality short-form video requires a good camera, decent lighting, basic editing skills, and time — none of which most small business owners have in abundance. A 30-second Reel that looks good can take 2–4 hours to shoot and edit properly.

**AI video generation** eliminates this barrier. You describe what you want, choose a format, and receive a professional video in minutes — no camera, no editing software, no creative experience required.

## The Production Problem for Small Businesses

Let's be realistic about what producing social media video actually costs.

**Video production agency:** €500–2,000 per video. High quality, but completely unaffordable for regular content creation at the frequency social media requires.

**Freelance videographer:** €100–300 per video. More accessible, but still expensive for weekly content. Coordination, scheduling, and revisions add time and cost.

**DIY filming:** Free, but requires equipment (smartphone alone is often insufficient), lighting, a quiet location, and editing skills most business owners don't have. Time cost is high.

**AI video generation:** €0.30 per 5-second video, €0.60 per 10-second video. No equipment, no scheduling, no editing — just a description and a style choice.

For a small business creating 4–8 social media videos per month, the math is clear.

## VendShop's 7 Social Media Presets

Rather than making you figure out aspect ratios and durations for every platform, VendShop AI Studio includes 7 presets pre-configured for the most common social media formats.

### Instagram Reel (9:16, 5s)
The standard Reel format. Optimized for Instagram's full-screen vertical playback. Ideal for **product showcases**, limited offers, and quick announcements. 5 seconds is the sweet spot for scroll-stopping content that delivers a clear message before viewers swipe.

### Instagram Story (9:16, 5s)
Same vertical format as Reels but optimized for Story flow. Use for **behind-the-scenes moments**, daily specials, team introductions, and time-sensitive offers. Stories disappear after 24 hours, which creates urgency — you can generate a fresh one daily.

### Instagram Post (1:1, 5s)
Square format for the main Instagram feed. Short videos in the feed get significantly higher engagement than static images. Use for **product demos, before/after reveals**, and branded content that you want to live permanently on your profile.

### YouTube Shorts (9:16, 10s)
YouTube Shorts gives small businesses access to YouTube's massive search-based audience. The 10-second format allows for slightly more content — a fuller product explanation, a mini-tutorial, or a short testimonial concept. Great for **tutorials, how-to content**, and evergreen posts that rank in search.

### TikTok (9:16, 10s)
TikTok's algorithm rewards authenticity and niche content. Small businesses in specific categories (food, beauty, automotive, health) consistently find engaged audiences. The 10-second format gives enough time for a punchy hook and a clear message. Best for **trending formats, storytelling**, and showing personality.

### Cinematic (16:9, 10s)
Horizontal widescreen format — the classic video format for website headers, presentation backgrounds, and YouTube mid-roll content. Use for **brand films, website background videos**, and content destined for screens rather than phones. Gives your business a premium, professional feel.

### Product Showcase (1:1, 5s)
Square format specifically designed for e-commerce and service presentation. Optimized for showing products clearly in motion — ideal for **online stores, menu items, service packages**, and anything you want to highlight in a clean, commercial style.

## Tips for Effective AI Video Prompts

Good video prompts follow the same principles as good image prompts, with some additional considerations for motion.

**Describe motion explicitly.** "A pizza rotating slowly on a wooden board" tells the AI what movement to create. "A pizza" does not.

**Set the scene completely.** Include the setting, lighting, and mood. "A cozy Italian restaurant kitchen, warm amber lighting, wooden surfaces" gives the AI much more to work with than "restaurant."

**Be specific about the subject.** "A golden espresso shot being poured into a white ceramic cup, close-up" produces better results than "coffee."

**Think about the first frame.** Since the video starts from a single image, describe what that first frame should look like — the AI animates from there.

**Match energy to platform.** TikTok content can be more dynamic and kinetic. Cinematic format should be slower and more deliberate. Keep the platform's natural content rhythm in mind.

## What to Create for Your Business

**Restaurant or café:** Signature dish close-up rotating in warm light. Chef hands at work plating food. Steaming coffee or cocktail pour. These food content types reliably generate high engagement on all platforms.

**Barbershop or salon:** Hair transformation reveal concept. Style close-up with clean lighting. The "reveal" format — even suggested in AI video — performs consistently well.

**Retail or e-commerce:** Product showcase at multiple angles. Seasonal collection highlight. New arrival announcement with product in motion.

**Service business:** Before/after concept. Team at work. Location walkthrough for first-time customers who want to know what to expect.
