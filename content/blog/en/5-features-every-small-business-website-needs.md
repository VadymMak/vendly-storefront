The bar for a "professional" website has risen dramatically over the past few years. Customers now expect speed, mobile optimization, and trust signals as a baseline — not a luxury.

Here are the 5 features your small business website absolutely needs in 2026.

## 1. Mobile-First Design

Over **65% of web traffic** comes from mobile devices. If your website doesn't look and work perfectly on a smartphone, you're losing more than half your potential customers before they even read your offer.

Mobile-first means:
- Text readable without zooming
- Buttons large enough to tap accurately
- Images that load fast on mobile networks
- Checkout flow that works one-handed

Every VendShop template is designed mobile-first and tested across 20+ devices.

## 2. WhatsApp Integration

In Central Europe (SK, CZ, DE, UA), WhatsApp is the primary communication channel for small businesses. Customers expect to be able to message you directly.

A well-placed WhatsApp button on your website converts visitors who aren't ready to "buy now" but want to ask a question first. Studies show this can increase conversions by **15–30%** for service businesses.

## 3. Fast Loading Speed

Google's Core Web Vitals are now a ranking factor. A site that loads in under 2 seconds ranks higher and converts better than a slow one.

Key optimizations that VendShop handles automatically:
- Image compression and WebP conversion
- Code splitting and lazy loading
- Edge CDN delivery via Vercel

## 4. AI Chatbot or Smart FAQ

Customers have questions at 11pm when you're not available. An AI chatbot or well-structured FAQ answers the most common questions automatically — reducing your support load and keeping potential customers engaged.

> A chatbot on your product pages can answer "Do you ship to Germany?" or "What sizes are available?" instantly, 24/7.

## 5. Multilingual Support

If your business serves — or could serve — customers in multiple languages, having your website available in those languages is a direct revenue driver.

This is especially true for businesses near borders (SK/CZ/AT/HU), businesses targeting German-speaking tourists, or international sellers.
